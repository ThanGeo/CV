Research Focus & Summary

I am a database systems researcher with extensive experience in designing database features and developing scalable data management solutions. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in the DIMA group at TU Berlin & BIFOLD, working on NebulaStream, an open-source Stream Processing Engine (SPE) for the Cloud-Edge continuum.

I received my PhD, titled "Scalable Management of Complex Spatial Data Types," from the University of Ioannina, where I focused on developing scalable solutions for big spatial data. I am also the principal designer and developer of Hecatoncheir, a high-performance, distributed spatial data–management library built in C++ with MPI.

Key Research Areas

  • Stream Processing: resource-efficient data structures, memory optimization, declarative query processing
  • Spatial Data Management: spatial query optimization, complex spatial data types, polygon approximations, distributed geospatial data management
  • Database Systems: indexing, execution engine, query optimization, concurrency control
